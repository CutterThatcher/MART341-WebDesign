The University of Montana Football Team, known as the Grizzlies, boasts a storied history and a legacy of success in collegiate football. One of the team's crowning achievements includes winning the NCAA Division I-AA National Championship in 1995, marking a pinnacle moment in the program's history. The Grizzlies have consistently been a force to be reckoned with in the Big Sky Conference, clinching multiple conference titles and making numerous appearances in the Football Championship Subdivision (FCS) playoffs. The team's home games at the Washington-Grizzly Stadium are legendary for their passionate fan base and the electric atmosphere that permeates the venue.



Several standout athletes have left an enduring impact on the University of Montana Football program. Notably, former quarterback Dave Dickenson is regarded as one of the greatest players in Grizzlies history. Dickenson led the team to the aforementioned national championship in 1995 and went on to have a successful professional career in the Canadian Football League (CFL). Other notable Grizzlies alumni include linebacker Kroy Biermann, who went on to have a successful career in the National Football League (NFL). These athletes, among others, have contributed to the rich tapestry of success for the University of Montana Football Team, leaving an indelible mark on the program's history.
